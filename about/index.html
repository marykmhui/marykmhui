About

Mary Hui has been at Quartz since 2019. She has reported extensively on the Hong Kong protests and China's ensuing crackdown. Currently, her coverage focuses on the intersection of geopolitics, supply chains, and industrial policy, and how the strategic competition between the US and China plays out in those domains.

Before joining Quartz, she worked as a freelance reporter, mostly covering socioeconomic issues in Hong Kong. Between 2016 and 2017, she spent 10 months at the Washington Post as an intern reporter on the local desk, focusing on social issues. She has a bachelor's degree in Spanish and politics from Princeton University, and attended Li Po Chun United World College for high school.
